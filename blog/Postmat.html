Post matrimonial investigation refers to the process of investigating a spouse's behavior after marriage. The reasons for post matrimonial investigation may vary from case to case, but some common reasons include:

  1. Infidelity: One of the most common reasons for post matrimonial investigation is suspected infidelity. If a spouse suspects that their partner is having an affair, they may hire a private investigator to gather evidence to confirm or refute their suspicions.
  2. Behavior: Sometimes, a spouse's behavior can raise suspicions even if there is no clear evidence of infidelity. For example, if a spouse suddenly starts working late, becomes secretive about their phone or computer, or exhibits other unusual behaviors, the other spouse may hire an investigator to find out what's going on.
  3. Child custody: If a couple is going through a divorce and there is a dispute over child custody, one spouse may hire a private investigator to gather evidence that could be used in court to support their case.
  4. Financial fraud: In some cases, one spouse may suspect that the other is hiding assets, engaging in financial fraud, or otherwise mismanaging finances. A private investigator can help uncover any suspicious financial activity and gather evidence that can be used in court.
  5. Substance abuse: If a spouse suspects that their partner is struggling with substance abuse, they may hire an investigator to gather evidence that can be used to convince their partner to seek help or to build a case for custody of their children.
  6. Personal safety: In some cases, a spouse may feel threatened or unsafe in their marriage and may hire an investigator to gather evidence of abuse or other behavior that could be used to obtain a restraining order or file for divorce.

Overall, the reasons for post matrimonial investigation are varied and depend on the specific circumstances of each case. It is important to consult with a legal professional before hiring a private investigator to ensure that the investigation is conducted legally and ethically.
